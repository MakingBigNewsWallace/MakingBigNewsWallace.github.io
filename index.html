About Me

Hi I'm Wenbo Gou*, actively looking for PhD positionstrong>! Currently, I'm a research assistant at Carnegie Mellon University (CMU) Robotics Institute (RI) working in the Human Sensing Lab with Prof. Fernando De la Torre. I obtained my MS ECE degree at CMU in May 2024, and I joined Human Sensing Lab since July 2023.

Before I came to CMU, I worked with Prof. Ruimao Zhang at The Chinese University of Hong Kong (Shenzhen). I earned a bachelor's degree in Electronic Information Engineering at Huazhong University of Science and Technology (HUST).

My current research interest lies in human-centric 3D Vision, including pose estimation/ mesh recovery, digital avatar reconstruction, and human-object interaction.

Projects

Hamba: Single-view 3D Hand Reconstruction with Graph-guided Bi-Scanning Mamba(NeurIPS 24)

Wenbo Gou*, Haoye Dong*, Aviral Chharia*, Francisco Vicente Carrasco, Fernando De la Torre

Combining Mamba state space model and graph neural network to reconstruct 3D hand mesh. Check our project webpage: Hamba.

Realtime Monocular View 3D Human Pose Estimation System(Work at HSL)

Wenbo Gou*, Haoye Dong*, Francisco Vicente Carrasco, Fernando De la Torre

Combining body, face, and hands pose estimation, creating a real-time robust whole body pose estimation and mesh recovery system

FreeMan: Towards benchmarking 3D HPE in the wild(CVPR 24)

Jiong WANG*, Fengyu Yang*, Wenbo Gou*, Bingliang Li, Danqi Yan, Ailing Zeng, Yijun Gao, Junle Wang, Yanqing Jing, Ruimao Zhang

The first large-scale multiview human pose dataset. In charge of single-view Human Pose Estimation (HPE) and Mesh Recovery (HMR) benchmarking section. Learn more about this project on the Freeman Project Page.
